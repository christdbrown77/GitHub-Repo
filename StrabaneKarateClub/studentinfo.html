Dojo Ettiquette

A full list of Dojo Rules can be seen on our notice board. However the following is a brief summary.Donâ€™t worry if your child does not pick them up quickly and please do not be too hard on the young children. They will soon learn the rules through the class. We want them to have fun and enjoy karate but through training begin to understand the discipline and focus required.

  • Karate students must be prompt and on time for training. Try to arrive to class 5-10 minutes early. If late, the student must kneel on the side of the mat and await the instructor to welcome them to the class.

  • Karate students should bow when entering or leaving the dojo, at the beginning and the end of class, before and after sparring with a partner, before and after performing a kata, and when greeting an instructor.

  • Karate students must remove all jewelry, watches, earrings, rings prior to training. These items can cause injury to oneself and others. If a piece of jewelry cannot be removed, then tape must be wrapped around it for protection.

  • Karate students must follow the instructions of their seniors and instructors, quickly and respectfully.

  • Karate students must not engage in idle talk or misbehave while training is in session. When not exercising students must remain attentive.

  • Karate students must have the instructor's permission to leave class before it has ended. If a Student needs to go to the toilet then they should raise their hand and ask an instructor.

  • Karate students must be courteous and helpful to each other.

Dress Code

New comers to the club may wear a Traditional White Karate Gi (Uniform), or loose comfortable clothing such as sweat pants and a thick t-shirt. For Beginners, we advise trying classes and be sure Karate is for you before purchasing a Gi. However after 3 to 4 weeks we expect all students to come in a Traditional White Karate Gi. These are available to purchase from the club. Please ask an Instructor for details. Current Members are expected to wear a Karate Gi at all classes with the exception of competition Kumite classes.

Key Terminology

Dojo Kun

The Dojo Kun is a set of precepts (Principles) by which a Karateka should not only observe within the Dojo but in life.
